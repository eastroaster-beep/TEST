FAQ

Common Questions Answered

Everything you need to know before opening your account. Still have questions? Our team is available 24/7.

Is Interactive Brokers suitable for beginners?

Absolutely. While IBKR's full platform (Trader Workstation) caters to professionals, the IBKR GlobalTrader and IBKR Mobile apps are purpose-built for newer investors. They offer simplified interfaces, educational tools via IBKR Campus, and paper trading accounts so you can practice with virtual money before committing real capital. You can graduate to more advanced tools as you grow. There is no account minimum to get started.

What types of accounts can I open at Interactive Brokers?

IBKR offers a wide range of account types to fit your situation: Individual & Joint brokerage accounts, Retirement accounts (Traditional IRA, Roth IRA, SEP IRA, SIMPLE IRA), Trust accounts, Corporate & LLC accounts, Family Office accounts, and even Investment Advisor (RIA) accounts to manage clients. All accounts are multi-currency and can access global markets simultaneously.

How does IBKR compare on commissions to competitors like Schwab or Fidelity?

For US stocks, IBKR Lite offers $0 commissions (like Schwab/Fidelity), while IBKR Pro charges $0.005/share — actually cheaper for larger trades. The real advantage emerges in international markets and options: IBKR's options commissions ($0.15–$0.65/contract) are consistently the lowest available. For forex, IBKR charges near-interbank spreads (0.08–0.20 bps) versus typical retail spreads of 1–3 pips at other brokers. Independent studies consistently rank IBKR as the #1 lowest-cost broker globally for active traders.

Is my money safe? What protections do I have?

Your assets are protected through multiple layers: SIPC insurance covers up to $500,000 in securities ($250,000 cash). Beyond this, IBKR carries excess insurance through Lloyd's of London, providing up to $30 million per account (with a $900M aggregate limit across all accounts). Additionally, Interactive Brokers LLC maintains over $12.5 billion in equity capital — far exceeding regulatory requirements. The firm has never had a down quarter in its 25+ year history as a public broker-dealer.
